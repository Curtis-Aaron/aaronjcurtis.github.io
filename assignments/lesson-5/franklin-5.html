Franklin Reservoirs

Reservoirs in Franklin County will be open to public use Memorial Day weekend. The Consolidated Irrigation Company will follow the same action taken during the previous seasons to prevent the spread and risk of infestation by the Quagga and Zebra mussel. “The board of directors, at the direction of our shareholders, have not taken this risk lightly. We have proceeded with the combined goal of protecting water resources and infrastructure for its intended agricultural uses,” states a release from the Consolidated Irrigation Company (CIC). CIC owns and operates four private reservoirs in Franklin County: Glendale, Johnson, Lamont, and Foster. Their purpose is to store water for irrigation for agricultural needs. The Idaho Department of Agriculture Boat Check Station on the Utah/Idaho boarder in Franklin opened on March 30, and are open from daylight to dusk all season.
